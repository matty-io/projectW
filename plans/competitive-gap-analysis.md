# Competitive Gap Analysis (market research, 2026-06-20)

Research into the category leaders (Wati, AiSensy, Interakt, Gallabox) and the Shopify-commerce layer,
against our target segment (India D2C, coaching, clinics, real estate — ARCHITECTURE §1).

## Headline finding
Everything built so far (broadcasts, templates, shared inbox, contacts, analytics) is the **"WhatsApp
broadcaster" baseline**. Every competitor has that *and* an automation/commerce layer on top. The
features below are largely **table stakes** now, not luxuries — the ones stuck at broadcasting are the
budget tier.

## The six growth gaps (with full plans)
| # | Gap | Why it matters | Impact | Effort | Plan |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No-code flow builder | Category's #1 buying criterion; foundation for automation + AI | ★★★★★ | L–XL | [growth-02-flow-builder](growth-02-flow-builder.md) |
| 2 | WhatsApp Flows (native forms) | Booking/lead-gen; Meta hosts rendering → best ROI:effort | ★★★★ | S–M | [growth-01-whatsapp-flows](growth-01-whatsapp-flows.md) |
| 3 | Shopify + abandoned-cart recovery | Direct ROI story for the core D2C target | ★★★★★ | M–L | [growth-03-shopify-cart-recovery](growth-03-shopify-cart-recovery.md) |
| 4 | Public API + webhooks + Zapier/Make | Unlocks long-tail integrations cheaply; qualifying criterion | ★★★ | M | [growth-04-integrations-api-zapier](growth-04-integrations-api-zapier.md) |
| 5 | CTWA attribution | Wins the performance-marketer D2C buyer | ★★★ | M | [growth-05-ctwa-attribution](growth-05-ctwa-attribution.md) |
| 6 | AI agent (LLM/Claude) | Differentiation frontier; builds on #1 | ★★★★ | L | [growth-06-ai-agent](growth-06-ai-agent.md) |

## Recommended build order & rationale
**#2 WhatsApp Flows first** (fast, high-visibility win) → **#1 Flow builder** (strategic centerpiece) →
**#3 Shopify/cart recovery** (D2C revenue hook) → **#4 integrations** → **#5 CTWA** → **#6 AI agent**.
Dependencies: #1 is the foundation #6 builds on; #5 consumes events from #1/#3.

## Source caveat
Most evidence is from vendor comparison/marketing pages, which are biased. The **direction** (flow
builder + Flows + commerce + AI as table stakes) is consistent across all of them; treat specific
numbers (Wati ~200-step flows, ~300% form-completion lift, ~30% cart recovery) as **vendor claims**,
not independent benchmarks.
